PROCESS

Screening Process

Coconut shell charcoal is fed into a sieving machine. The purpose of this process is to separate the ash from the combustion to maintain the quality of the briquettes, and ash shouldn't be included.

Grinding Process

After going through the sifting process, the coconut shell charcoal is put into a grinding machine. This process is carried out until the charcoal turns to be powder.

Mixing Process

The charcoal powder is put into the mixing machine with the starch. Starch serves as an adhesive in briquettes. The ratio of charcoal powder and starch is 4: 1. This process is taking time for 10 minutes and make sure that the ingredients are well mixed.

Blending Process

The next step is blending where the ingredients are put into a blending machine until being into a dough and let it cool down.

Molding process

In this step, the dough is fed into the molding machine. Shapes and sizes can be made to order.

Furnacing Process

Last step, the briquettes that have been formed are put in an oven at 100°c and let it for 38 hours. after being removed from the oven, the briquettes must be aired all day. the briquettes can be packaged.
